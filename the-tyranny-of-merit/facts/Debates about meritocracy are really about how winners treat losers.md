#fact in [[2 - Introduction]]

On the surface, public arguments about [[Meritocracy]] read as arguments about [[Equality|fairness]]: whether the contest is rigged, whether the rules are even, whether the playing field tilts. Underneath, the real question is about [[Winner|winners and losers]], and what the winners owe to the losers, and how they are entitled to act toward them.

Reframed this way, "fixing" [[Meritocracy]] by making the competition more [[Equality|equal]] does not resolve the deeper problem: even a perfectly fair contest produces a [[Hubris of Meritocratic Success|hierarchy of moral worth]] that erodes [[Civic Sensibility]].
