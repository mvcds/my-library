[[The populist backlash stems from economic disparity and powerlessness|Democracy is in danger]], and populist [[anger]] is its symptom. [[Public Backlash|Mainstream parties read the backlash]] as a reaction to diversity and to workers' [[Success|failure]] to adapt to [[Globalization]] which is partly true, but a diagnosis that conveniently leaves the parties themselves out of the picture.

What the diagnosis hides is the parties' own forty-year [[Meritocratic Project]], propped up by [[Technocracy brackets moral arguments out of politics|technocracy]]: a faith in [[market]] efficiency that brackets [[Moral|moral]] argument out of [[politics]] and reserves judgement for [[Credentials|credentialed]] experts. Market-driven globalization, in turn, [[Globalization weakened mutual dependence on local economies|loosened the ties]] that held local [[Tribe|groups]] together.

This is why the political divide no longer maps cleanly onto right vs left, but onto [[Open vs Closed|globalists vs tribalists]].
