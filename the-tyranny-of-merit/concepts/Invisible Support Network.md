#concept in [[1 - Prologue]]

The conditions that make individual [[Achieving Goals|achievement]] possible but stay invisible to the person who achieves it:

- [[institution|institutions]] and [[Common Good|public goods]][^3]
- family circumstances[^2]
- mentors and teachers[^1]
- [[Probability|luck]] of [[time|timing]]
- the labor of others

Those who land on top tend to attribute the outcome to their own [[Competence]] and [[effort]], not to the [[network]] that carried them there.

This blindness is the [[psychology|psychological]] engine of [[The tyranny of merit]]: if the support network is invisible, [[success]] looks fully deserved, and the failure of others looks fully their own fault.

[^1]: The [[Knowledge]] that has been accumulated and passed forward
[^2]: [[Money]], [[Social]] privileges, the place they were born, etc
[^3]: The [[Government]] probably helps a lot here
