#concept in [[2 - Introduction]]

The widening distance between quality of life, which usually starts with the [[knowledge|educational]] gap between top [[Common Good|public, elite universities]] (and the schools that feed them) and everywhere else.

As the educational gap grows, the consequences of landing on one side or the other compound: a degree is no longer one [[Credential]] among many but the gate to a separate trajectory.

This is the structural condition that enables [[The elite university admissions arms race]] to seems rational rather than paranoid, and that gives [[Meritocratic Anxiety]] its real-world bite.
