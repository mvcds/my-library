#concept in [[3 - Winners and Losers]]

The backbone of [[Democracy]] is the people voting, but the public has lashed out against the [[Government]], and mainstream parties explain the [[The populist backlash stems from economic disparity and powerlessness|populist backlash]] with two stories:

- [[Discrimination intertwines with identity|diversity]] is unsettling the default male who feels he is losing his privileges
- [[Globalization]] — through outsourcing and [[Technology]] — has taken jobs from workers unwilling to adapt to the new world

These diagnoses are partly true, but they let the parties themselves off the hook. They ignore that the same mainstream has spent forty years cooking the conditions for the backlash through a [[Technocracy brackets moral arguments out of politics|technocratic]] conception of the [[Common Good|public good]] and a [[Meritocratic Project|meritocratic]] sorting of [[Winner|winners and losers]].
