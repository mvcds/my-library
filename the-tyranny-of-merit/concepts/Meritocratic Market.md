#concept in [[3 - Winners and Losers]]

The mainstream parties' forty-year [[Reagan and Thatcher set the unchallenged market consensus|political project]] of defining [[Winner|winners and losers]] by [[Meritocracy|merit]] while keeping the market-consensus intact. The center-left softened its edges and decorated it with a discourse of [[Moral|moral]] and [[Civic Sensibility|civic]] duty that rarely survives contact with [[Government|governing]][^1].

Paired with [[Technocracy brackets moral arguments out of politics|technocracy]], it reframes who deserves what in [[society]] as the verdict of a fair contest, which makes the [[Hubris of Meritocratic Success|winners feel entitled]] and [[Individualization of Failure|blames]] the losers.

[^1]: See [[Obama bailed banks but not homeowners]].
